<p><p>Ammonia-fuelled engines are categorized into three main types based on their ignition mechanisms: Spark-Ignition Engine, Compression-Ignition Engine, and Gas-Turbine Engine. Spark-Ignition Engines use a spark plug to ignite the ammonia-air mixture, while Compression-Ignition Engines rely on the high temperature generated by compressing the ammonia-air mixture to ignite the fuel. Gas-Turbine Engines combust the ammonia-air mixture in a continuous process to generate power. Each type of engine has unique characteristics and applications within the broader ammonia-fuelled engine market.</p></p>
