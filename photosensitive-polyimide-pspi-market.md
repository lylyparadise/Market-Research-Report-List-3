<p><ul><li>Positive Photosensitive Polyimide</li><li>Negative Photosensitive Polyimide</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Photosensitive Polyimide (PSPI) is a type of material used in various industries for its photoresist properties. Positive Photosensitive Polyimide refers to a type of PSPI that becomes soluble when exposed to light, allowing for selective removal during the development process. On the other hand, Negative Photosensitive Polyimide becomes insoluble when exposed to light, making the exposed areas remain after development. Both types of PSPI have their own applications and advantages in industries such as electronics, aerospace, and automotive.</p></p>
