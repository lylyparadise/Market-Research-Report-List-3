<p><p>Camera applications in microscopy include CCD (Charge-Coupled Device) cameras and CMOS (Complementary Metal-Oxide-Semiconductor) cameras. CCD cameras are known for their high sensitivity and low noise, making them ideal for capturing low-light images with high resolution. On the other hand, CMOS cameras are more cost-effective and power-efficient, making them popular for real-time imaging applications. Both types of cameras have their unique strengths and are widely used in various microscopy applications for research, healthcare, and industrial purposes.</p></p>
