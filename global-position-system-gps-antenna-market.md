<p><p>GPS antennas are divided into two main types: internal antennas and external antennas. Internal antennas are built into devices such as smartphones, car navigation systems, and fitness trackers. They are less obtrusive and generally have limited range compared to external antennas. External antennas are separate devices that are mounted externally on vehicles, buildings, or other structures. They provide better reception and accuracy as they are not limited by the device's size or placement. Both types cater to different needs and preferences in the GPS antenna market.</p></p>
