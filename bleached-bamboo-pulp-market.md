<p><ul><li>Sulfate Process</li><li>Caustic Soda Process</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Bleached bamboo pulp is produced through two main processes: sulfate process and caustic soda process. Sulfate process involves treating bamboo fibers with sulfuric acid and sodium hydroxide to break them down into pulp, while caustic soda process involves using sodium hydroxide to dissolve the fibers. Both processes result in bleached bamboo pulp which is used in various industries such as paper, textiles, and packaging. The choice between the two processes depends on factors such as cost, environmental impact, and desired pulp properties.</p></p>
