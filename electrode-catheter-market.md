There are two main types of electrode catheters: Floating Electrode Catheters and Common Electrode Catheters. </p><p>Floating Electrode Catheters have multiple electrodes along the catheter shaft which provides more contact points for mapping and ablation. Common Electrode Catheters have a single electrode at the tip and are commonly used for diagnostic purposes. Both types of catheters are essential tools for cardiac electrophysiologists in guiding treatment and improving patient outcomes.</p></p>
