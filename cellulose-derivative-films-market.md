<p><p>Cellulose derivative films can be categorized into two main types: coated films and uncoated films. Coated films are cellulose derivative films that have been treated with additional substances, such as polymers or chemicals, to enhance their properties like moisture resistance or barrier properties. On the other hand, uncoated films are cellulose derivative films that have not undergone any additional treatments and retain the natural characteristics of cellulose. Both types of films are widely used in various industries for packaging, labeling, and other applications.</p></p>
