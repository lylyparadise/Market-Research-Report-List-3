<p><p>Refractory clay can be categorized into two main types - hard clay and soft clay. Hard clay is known for its high refractoriness, strength, and abrasion resistance, making it suitable for applications that require high temperatures and mechanical stress. Soft clay, on the other hand, has lower refractoriness but offers better thermal shock resistance. It is commonly used in applications where rapid temperature changes occur frequently. Both types of refractory clay play a crucial role in various industries such as steel, glass, and cement production.</p></p>
