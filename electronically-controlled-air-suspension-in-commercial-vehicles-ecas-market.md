<p><p>Electronically Controlled Air Suspension (ECAS) in commercial vehicles refers to a system that automatically adjusts the vehicle's ride height and stiffness based on road conditions and load. This system provides a smoother and more stable ride, improving driver comfort and vehicle performance. On the other hand, non-electronically controlled air suspension relies on manual adjustment of air pressure in the air springs. While still offering benefits such as improved ride quality and load-carrying capabilities, it lacks the dynamic adaptability of ECAS systems.</p></p>
