<p><p>Retinoblastoma treatment market types include congenital (hereditary) and sporadic (non-hereditary) retinoblastoma. Congenital retinoblastoma is caused by an inherited genetic mutation, typically affecting both eyes and occurring at an early age. In contrast, sporadic retinoblastoma develops in children without a family history of the disease and usually affects only one eye. These distinctions are important in determining the appropriate treatment approach for each type of retinoblastoma.</p></p>
