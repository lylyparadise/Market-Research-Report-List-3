<p><p>Healthcare microfluidics market consists of glass-based, polymer-based, silicon-based, and other types of microfluidic devices. Polymer-based microfluidics provide flexibility and cost-effectiveness, making them suitable for point-of-care diagnostics. Silicon-based microfluidics offer precise control and integration with electronics, suitable for lab-on-a-chip applications. Other types of microfluidic devices include hybrid materials or specialty designs catering to specific healthcare needs. Each type has its unique advantages and applications in the healthcare industry.</p></p>
