<p><p>Railroad weighing scales can be classified into two main types: static and dynamic. Static scales are stationary and used to weigh rail cars while they are motionless. Dynamic scales, on the other hand, are designed to weigh rail cars while they are moving. Static scales are commonly used for accurate weight measurements of stationary rail cars, while dynamic scales are preferred for efficiency in weighing rail cars as they are in motion, allowing for quicker and more seamless operations.</p></p>
